# Exbabel Marketing Brand Guidelines

**Target Audience:** Marketing Directors, Brand Managers, and External Partners  
**Version:** 1.0 (2026)

---

## 1. Brand Identity & Mission

**Our Mission**  
*Speak Once. Reach Every Language.*

Exbabel transforms any church livestream, conference, or event into a multilingual experience in real time using AI-powered voice translation and live captions. We build a more welcoming, accessible, and unified church experience.

**Core Narrative**  
We eliminate the need for traditional interpreters, separate language services, and additional broadcasts. It’s **One Service. Unlimited Languages.**

---

## 2. Voice & Tone

Our communication is built on the philosophy of **"Calm clarity under cognitive load. Premium, consistent, and mathematically sound."**

*   **Verifiable Proof over Marketing Noise:** We don't exaggerate. We rely on facts, impact, and verifiable metrics.
*   **Metric Dignity:** Present statistics and impact clearly. Never hide the numbers. Use large scales and semantic eyebrows to communicate scale.
*   **Honest Integrations:** Instead of claiming "Trusted By," we state "Works Seamlessly With" (e.g., OBS, vMix, Wirecast).
*   **Human + AI Narrative:** The technology is advanced, but the goal is deeply human: inclusion. The technology should disappear into the background so people simply feel included.

---

## 3. Design Philosophy: The "Aurora" Language

Our visual identity moves beyond flat UI into a system built on **layers, depth, and light**—the Aurora Design System.

*   **No Dead White:** White is never just flat color. It is a quiet canvas that reacts to light.
*   **Light as Feedback:** Interaction is communicated through the emergence of light (gradients and glows) rather than just flat color changes.
*   **Calm Shell, Energetic Core:** Components appear calm and neutral at rest but reveal their "brand energy" (Aurora gradients) upon interaction.

---

## 4. Color Palette

We use the perceptually uniform OKLCH color space for perfect contrast and vibrance.

### Foundation Colors
*   **Surface (White):** `#FFFFFF` — For cards and main content areas.
*   **Paper (Background):** `#F8F9FA` — Used for page backgrounds to prevent "dead white."
*   **Ink (Primary Text/Buttons):** `#0B1220` — High contrast, premium anchor color.
*   **Muted (Secondary Text):** `#667085` — For secondary information and less prominent text.
*   **Primary Action (Blue):** `#3B5BDB` (`oklch(0.608 0.370 264.7)`) — Vibrant Blue for brand actions and links.

### The Aurora Gradients ("Brand Energy")
These colors form the "Aurora" effect. They are rarely used as solid blocks, but rather as gradients, blurs, and glows to inject life and interaction into the interface.
*   **Pink (`#FFD6E5`):** Active states, warm highlights, the "heartbeat."
*   **Purple (`#EAD6FF`):** Primary ambient light, structural depth.
*   **Mint (`#D6F5FF`):** Cool focus, clarity, tech precision.
*   **Yellow (`#FFF7D1`):** Subtle warmth, tertiary accents.

---

## 5. Typography System

Our typography reflects our "Human + AI" narrative.

*   **Headings: Sora**  
    Sora provides a unique, friendly, yet technical character fitting for our advanced AI but human-centered application.
*   **Body Copy: Inter**  
    Inter ensures perfect legibility for UI text and long-form reading content.

### Typographic Rhythm
*   **H1 (Display):** ExtraBold. Used for Hero Sections.
*   **H2:** Bold. Used for Section Titles.
*   **H3:** SemiBold. Used for Card Titles and Feature Heads.
*   **Eyebrow:** Bold, Uppercase, tracking-wide. Used to anchor sections and announce categories before the main heading.

---

## 6. Layout & Visual Rhythm

*   **The 8pt Grid:** We eradicate "magic numbers" in favor of a strict 8-point mathematical scale for spacing. Everything is intentional and consistent.
*   **Continuous Flow:** Avoid hard section dividers. Use transparent backgrounds and overlapping aurora blobs (ambient background gradients) to bridge transitions softly.

---

## 7. Component Styling

*   **The "Aurora Button":** Pill-shaped (`rounded-full`). At rest, it has a solid Ink shell. On hover, it reveals the Aurora Gradient (Pink → Mint) bleeding through via a pseudo-element glow. No jumpy animations; use a premium `scale-[1.02]` lift.
*   **Cards ("Lifted by Light"):** Cards don't use harsh cartoon borders. They use a subtle purple-tinted border with a colored, diffused shadow. On interaction, the card lifts slightly and the light-bleed shadow intensifies.

---

## Marketing Director's Checklist for New Collateral

> [!IMPORTANT]
> When reviewing new landing pages, sales decks, or ad creatives, ensure they meet the following baseline criteria:

- [ ] Is the primary message centered on inclusion and "One Service"?
- [ ] Does the copy rely on "Verifiable Proof" rather than fluff?
- [ ] Are headings in **Sora** and body text in **Inter**?
- [ ] Do interactive elements utilize the "Aurora" glow/light-bleed instead of flat color swaps?
- [ ] Is the layout continuous, avoiding harsh flat dividers?
- [ ] Are numbers and impact metrics given "Metric Dignity" (large scale + eyebrow)?
