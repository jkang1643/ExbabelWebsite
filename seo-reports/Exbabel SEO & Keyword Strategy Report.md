# Exbabel SEO & Keyword Strategy Report

**Executive Summary:** Exbabel is a real-time AI translation platform tailored for live events and worship services. Its homepage emphasizes browser-based access, QR-code joins, and support for 150+ languages. Competitors in this niche include Wordly, CaptionKit, OneAccord, and Glossa, each focusing on live church translation but with different angles (e.g. Wordly on enterprise, CaptionKit on captions, OneAccord on “humanized” AI, Glossa on voice and price). This report provides a deep keyword and content strategy to help Exbabel capture high-intent searches, fill gaps where competitors lag, and guide users from awareness to trial/demo signup. It includes prioritized keyword lists by intent, keyword-to-page mapping (with URL slugs, title tags, H1s, meta descriptions, CTAs, internal links), comparison-page keyword sets (Exbabel vs each competitor), and a 3-month content calendar. We also include a conversion-path flowchart (mermaid) and KPIs to measure success.

**Current Position:** Exbabel’s homepage (Exbabel.com) pitches “150+ languages” real-time translation for events and worship. It highlights *no hardware needed* (just any browser), instant QR-code access, and free trial with 4 hours of captions/translations. Key selling points are “real-time AI translation that understands context, culture, and nuance”, plus features like 24/7 support and unlimited users. However, all content is currently on one page. For SEO, Exbabel should break out core topics into dedicated pages (e.g. “Live Captions”, “Audio Translation”, “Video Translation”, “QR Code Access”, “Pricing/Demo”), so it can target specific search queries more effectively.

## Competitor Analysis 

We reviewed the official sites of the top competitors (Wordly, CaptionKit, OneAccord, Glossa) to understand their messaging, keywords, and gaps. Key findings:

- **Wordly (wordly.ai)** – Broad, enterprise-leaning solution also used by houses of worship. Their *Church Translation* page emphasizes **“Live AI translation & captioning”** of services into both audio and text, with “dozens of language” options. It highlights **no special equipment needed** (“powered by AI, no sound booth or headsets”) and offers transcripts/summaries after each session. They note pricing by the hour and ~50–90% cost savings vs human interpreters. Key SEO keywords on Wordly’s site include “church translation”, “live translation and captioning”, “multilingual worship services”, and “accessible church services”. Wordly’s assets: comprehensive feature set (QR-code access, hybrid support, glossaries, onboarding) and trust signals (G2 ratings, compliance badges). A gap: Wordly targets all kinds of events (conferences, meetings) and thus uses broader language (e.g. “events”), so Exbabel can own the **“church-first”** angle more exclusively.

- **CaptionKit (captionkit.io)** – A startup focused specifically on church captions and translation. The homepage headline is **“Realtime captions and translations for your church”**, stressing accessibility (for Deaf/hard-of-hearing and multilingual attendees). It offers a **free 4-hour trial** and then plans “start at $16/month”. Features include multi-device QR-link access, display integration (ProPresenter/OBS), and customization (keyword boosting, profanity filters). CaptionKit emphasizes *no install needed*: “works in all modern browsers… no apps or downloads needed”. Key SEO terms are “live caption translations church”, “downloadable transcripts”, “accessibility captions”, “multilingual worship app”. A potential gap: CaptionKit primarily markets captions (text) and Deaf/hard-of-hearing features, not voice translation for hearing attendees (though they do audio via app), so Exbabel should highlight full **audio+text solutions**. Also, CaptionKit’s pricing is low ($16 for 4h), but Exbabel can emphasize unlimited devices and scaling (unlimited users) for a similar price.

- **OneAccord (oneaccord.ai)** – Church-focused translation “with a human touch.” Their hero line is **“Live AI Translation for churches… built by church interpreters”**. They highlight **specialized AI models trained on biblical terminology** and an optional “Moderation” mode for leaders to approve transcripts. OneAccord offers captions + audio in 50+ languages, with features like branded URLs, ProPresenter/OBS integration, and downloadable transcripts. Pricing starts at **$150/month for 5 hours**. OneAccord’s SEO angle: “church translation software”, “sermon translation”, “Bible terminology AI”, “live translation for church services”. They emphasize *church credentials* (“interpreters, pastors”, Biblical language), which appeals to conservative congregations. A gap: OneAccord is relatively expensive and less self-serve (contact for custom quotes), whereas Exbabel can emphasize “transparent, no-credit-card trial and low-cost plans” (Exbabel’s $24 for 5h vs $150). Also, Exbabel can broaden to more than 50 languages (150+ claimed).

- **Glossa (glossa.live)** – Lightweight, voice-translation focus. Their tagline: **“Real-time AI Translation for Churches and Events…100+ languages. No equipment needed.”**. Key features: **voice cloning** (use the speaker’s voice in translation), ultra-fast AI (“fractions of a second”), and live streaming (RTMP) integration. Pricing is pay-as-you-go or plans ($5/hour or $99/month for 25 hours). Glossa’s SEO terms include “church voice translator”, “livestream translation”, “ProPresenter translations”, “OBS translation plugin”. They claim to be **affordable** (“less than a box of chocolates”), targeting cost-conscious groups. A gap: Glossa focuses on voice/audio; their site doesn’t emphasize captions or multilingual transcripts for the hearing-impaired. Exbabel can match Glossa’s tech (no hardware, cheap) and add features like captions, transcripts, and manage hybrid events (Wordly-style) to outflank Glossa. Also, Glossa’s content has very short copy (mostly features and pricing); Exbabel can win on content depth and church-specific messaging.

**Competitor Highlights:** 
- Wordly: “live translation of your services into both audio and text output”, easy QR access, transcripts/summaries, 50–90% cost savings.  
- CaptionKit: “Real-time captions and translations for your church”, “works in all browsers, no apps needed”, “free 4 hours; plans from $16/mo”.  
- OneAccord: “real-time sermon translation… with captions and translations in over 50 languages”, “no download needed… simply scan the QR code”, pricing “$150/month for 5 hours”.  
- Glossa: “AI Translation for Churches… 100+ languages. No equipment needed”, “Voice cloning – use your speaker’s voice”, “Pay As You Go: $5/hour, 100+ languages”.

*Implication for Exbabel:* The competitors confirm high intent around terms like **church translation, live captions, multilingual worship, real-time sermon translation**. Gaps/opportunities for Exbabel: emphasize **browser-only/QR-code access** (no app download), **24/7 support and easy setup** (Wordly’s 10-minute claim), and **video/livestream translation** (the user will add). Exbabel’s pricing ($24/mo starter for 5h) undercuts OneAccord and rivals Glossa’s affordability, which should be highlighted. Also, Exbabel’s 150+ languages exceed OneAccord and Glossa’s count, a strong point.

## Keyword Research and Clusters

Based on competitor terms and Exbabel’s USP, we prioritize keywords by search intent:

- **Commercial / Transactional:** High-value buyer queries. Examples: **“real-time AI translation for churches”**, **“live translation for church services”**, **“church translation services”**, **“church captioning app”**, **“church translation software pricing”**, **“multilingual church services solution”**. These align with searches from church tech decision-makers comparing solutions. (Search volume: unspecified; difficulty: medium-high due to established competitors.)  
- **Feature-specific:** Searches looking for particular capabilities. E.g. **“QR code church translation”**, **“no app church captioning”**, **“live captions for church service”**, **“audio interpretation for church”**, **“livestream video translation”**, **“Bible verses AI translation”**, **“ProPresenter church captions”**, **“downloadable sermon transcripts”**. These capture users who know what feature they need. They may discover Exbabel by specific features (QR code access, multi-language support, video support, etc.). Difficulty: lower-medium if niche.  
- **Comparison:** Terms where users compare tools. We will create pages targeting **“Exbabel vs Wordly”**, **“Exbabel vs CaptionKit”**, **“Exbabel vs OneAccord”**, **“Exbabel vs Glossa”** and related phrases like **“best church translation software”**, **“Wordly alternative”**, **“CaptionKit competitor”**. (Difficulty: high intent but competitor brand names make it targeted traffic.)  
- **Pricing / Demo:** Queries indicating readiness to purchase or try. E.g. **“church translation pricing”**, **“free church translation trial”**, **“church translation software demo”**, **“cost of live church translation”**, **“Exbabel pricing”**. These pages focus on Exbabel’s offer.  
- **Informational / Awareness:** Top-of-funnel educational queries. E.g. **“how to add live translation to church service”**, **“make church services multilingual”**, **“accessible church services AI captions”**, **“language barrier church solution”**, **“live audio vs captioning for worship”**, **“AI for sermon translation guide”**. These openers lead users into discovering Exbabel’s solution. Difficulty: medium-high, but useful to establish topical authority.

*(Search volumes are generally niche and not publicly available; we mark them unspecified. However, terms like “church translation software” and “live translation for church” are highly relevant and likely have moderate volume.)*

## Keyword Intent Clusters

1. **Commercial / Transactional (Purchase intent):**  
   - *Examples:* “church translation software”, “live translation for church services”, “multilingual worship translation”, “sermon translation service”, “AI translation for church”.  
   - *Goal:* Capture searchers ready to evaluate/buy a solution. Use pages like Church Translation, Pricing, Demo.  
   - *Ranking Goals:* Top 3 for head keywords like “church translation service”, “live church translation”, as these directly match Exbabel’s offerings (ex: Wordly ranks for these).  

2. **Feature Searches:**  
   - *Examples:* “QR code church translation”, “no app needed translation church”, “live captions worship service”, “voice translation church”, “livestream church translation”, “ProPresenter translation”.  
   - *Goal:* Educate users on specific features. Create dedicated pages (QR Code, Captions, Audio, Video) to rank for these. These terms often have lower volume but high conversion when matched.  
   - *Opportunity:* Competitors do not focus on *video/live streaming* much; Exbabel’s new Live Video page can fill this niche. Also target “browser-based translation”, “no hardware required” terms, as Exbabel emphasizes this.  

3. **Comparison Searches:**  
   - *Examples:* “Exbabel vs Wordly”, “Exbabel vs CaptionKit”, “best church translation software”.  
   - *Goal:* Attract buyers comparing options. Dedicated comparison pages will target these. Use competitor names and key differentiation points (e.g. Exbabel’s pricing and features vs theirs).  
   - *Opportunity:* Competitors rarely rank for “vs” pages; Exbabel can capture these searches by explicitly naming rivals and using exact phrases (e.g., “Exbabel vs OneAccord”).  

4. **Pricing/Transactional:**  
   - *Examples:* “church translation pricing”, “free trial AI translation”, “Exbabel cost”, “church service translation plan”.  
   - *Goal:* Convert highly-interested users. Emphasize pricing page and trial/demo.  
   - *Opportunity:* Since CaptionKit promotes “4 hours free”, Exbabel should emphasize its **4-hour free trial** and transparent pricing from $24 as a selling point.  

5. **Informational/Awareness:**  
   - *Examples:* “how to translate church services live”, “reach multilingual congregation”, “make church services accessible”, “AI captions for worship”.  
   - *Goal:* Educate and build SEO authority. These lead into Exbabel’s solution (via blog content or how-it-works pages).  
   - *Opportunity:* Cover topics like “benefits of live translation at church” or “live captions vs audio for accessibility.” This content can link to feature pages for conversion (e.g. “Live Captions” page).

## Content Gaps & Opportunities

From the competitor review, Exbabel can dominate areas where others are weak:

- **Video/Livestream Translation:** Competitors (except Wordly’s mention) have limited content on live video translation. Exbabel’s new **Live Video Translation** page should target “livestream translation” and “video captions/dubbing” keywords.  
- **Browser-Based Access (No App):** While competitors mention QR codes or “no download” (OneAccord, CaptionKit, Wordly), Exbabel should double down on easy web access (e.g. “just scan a QR code or link to join”). Emphasize *no apps, no installs* as a unique convenience.  
- **Multilingual Range:** With **150+ languages**, Exbabel surpasses OneAccord (50+) and Glossa (100+) and CaptionKit (85). This is an SEO hook: target terms like “church translation 150 languages” or “many language support”.  
- **Pricing Transparency:** OneAccord’s high pricing and Glossa’s pay-as-you-go are complicated; Exbabel can highlight its simple monthly tiers (starting at $24) and four-hour free trial. SEO targeting: “affordable church translation” could capture cost-sensitive searches.  
- **Worship Focus:** Competitors like Wordly and Glossa cater to conferences and events. Exbabel should emphasize “built for worship” in content (as OneAccord does with church interpreters) to attract faith-based organizations. Keywords like “church AI translation platform” can be used.  
- **Accessibility Features:** CaptionKit markets heavily to Deaf/hearing-impaired congregations. Exbabel should incorporate and SEO-optimize any accessibility features (e.g. “real-time captions for hearing-impaired congregations”).  
- **Integrations:** Competitors mention ProPresenter/OBS (OneAccord) or "display apps" (CaptionKit). If Exbabel plans similar capabilities, create content for them. If not, avoid these terms to not raise unmet expectations.
